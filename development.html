"Twelve Pillars" and My Understandings

-by Jim rohn and Chris Widener

Chapter 4

The major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it.

In chapter 4 they essentially talked about how goal setting is deciding where you want

to go and then designing a plan to get you there. Setting goals that stretch you and make you do things

differently is what will lead you to a future that you want. Don’t wait for people to set goals for you.

Without setting goals in my life I would not have reached where am today because i would not have

known what to work towards.

Chapter 5

Everyday has many opportunities, but only one best opportunity.

The pain of discipline weighs ounces while the pain of regret weighs tons.

You should spend your time on the opportunities that align with your overall goals.

Dont waste your time on trivial things but those that are important.

I have been taught this my entire life by my parents and are greatful to them

because it is a very important piece of advice that can put you on the right tracts.

Chapter 6

Don’t join an easy crowd; you wont grow. Go where the expectations and the demands to perform are high.

Identify the people around you, evaluate them and check to see what impact they are having on you.

Be mindful of the people around and make sure they are people that will push you, challenge you

and encourage you to become better. To attract attractive people, you must be attractive.

I have always followed these steps in life by distancing myself from negativity and gravitaing

towards positivity keeping in mind that i must treat other how i would like to be treated.

Chapter 7

Formal education will make you a living. Self education will make you a fortune.

Self-education is what you teach yourself. It is what you learn along the way so

that you are constantly improving and growing.

To separate yourself from everyone else, be focused on continuous learning.

Most of life is lived after your formal education. That means the rest of it is up to you.

To apply this, I spend the "free-time" I have to learn different things.
